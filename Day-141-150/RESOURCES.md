# Day 141-150 — Resources: Final Interview Blitz

## Primary (assigned)

- **Pramp.com** — free, structured peer mock interviews with a scheduling system; you're paired with another candidate and interview each other in the same slot. The assigned best resource for this block's core deliverable (10 mock interviews, at least 3 with real peers) — start here before looking anywhere else for a practice partner.

## Mock interview practice partners

- **DevOps-focused Discord communities** — several active DevOps/SRE/platform-engineering Discord servers run informal mock-interview pairing channels; genuinely useful because infra/platform system-design questions get much rarer coverage in generic prep (which defaults to "design Twitter"-style app questions) than a DevOps-specific community provides.
- **interviewing.io** — anonymized peer and paid professional mock interviews; free tiers exist alongside paid interviewer-of-choice matching.
- **r/devops, r/kubernetes, r/sre** — occasional "looking for a mock interview partner" threads; lower structure than Pramp, but a source of people specifically in DevOps/SRE/platform roles.
- **CNCF Slack / Kubernetes Slack community channels** — not built for mock interviews specifically, but active enough that a direct, polite ask for a practice partner often lands.

## STAR / behavioral interview prep

- **Amazon's own "STAR method" interview guidance** (search "Amazon interview STAR method") — Amazon popularized and formalized STAR most heavily in tech hiring; their public guidance is a clean, authoritative explanation of the format and what they listen for.
- **Google's Interview Warmup tool** (grow.google/certificates/interview-warmup) — free, gives you a low-stakes way to practice speaking behavioral answers out loud and get instant AI feedback on structure/pacing before you use a real peer's time.
- **"Cracking the Coding Interview" (behavioral chapter) / similar big-tech interview guides** — even though most of the book is algorithmic, the behavioral-question chapter and the general "tell me about a time" framing translate directly.

## Salary negotiation

- **levels.fyi** — crowdsourced total-compensation data (base + bonus + equity) by company, role, and level; the primary source for knowing your actual market number before any negotiation conversation.
- **Glassdoor / Blind (teamblind.com)** — supplementary comp and negotiation-experience data points, useful for cross-checking levels.fyi numbers and reading real negotiation anecdotes for specific companies.
- **Patrick McKenzie ("patio11") — "Salary Negotiation: Make More Money, Be More Valued"** — one of the most widely cited practical negotiation guides in tech; covers anchoring, why to avoid giving the first number, and how to negotiate the full package, not just base.
- **Haseeb Qureshi — negotiation writing (haseebq.com)** — additional practical, tech-industry-specific negotiation advice, including how to handle competing offers honestly and effectively.

## Portfolio and LinkedIn optimization

- **Tech Interview Handbook** (techinterviewhandbook.org) — free, has a dedicated resume/LinkedIn/portfolio optimization section alongside its better-known interview-prep content; the resume and behavioral sections are relevant even though the site leans generalist rather than DevOps-specific.
- **GitHub's own "About READMEs" / profile README docs** (docs.github.com) — the official reference for setting up a profile README and understanding what renders where; use it to confirm your profile page is actually configured correctly, not just written.
- **"How to write a great README" (various engineering blogs — search for it directly)** — general guidance that applies directly to polishing your pinned repos' READMEs per the checklist in file 3.
- **LinkedIn's own Creator/Profile optimization help pages** — official guidance on headline, About section length/structure, and visibility settings (open-to-work, recruiter visibility) referenced in this block's checklist.
