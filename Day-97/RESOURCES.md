# Day 97 — Resources: Incident Management

## Primary (assigned)

- **PagerDuty Incident Response Guide** (free PDF, response.pagerduty.com) — the assigned starting point for this day. A practical, field-tested guide covering severity classification, roles (including the Incident Commander concept), and communication practices used industry-wide.

## Deepen your understanding

- **Google SRE Book — Chapter 14: Managing Incidents** (sre.google/sre-book/managing-incidents, free online) — Google's own incident management framework, including the roles (IC, Ops Lead, Communications Lead) and why clear role separation matters at scale.
- **Google SRE Book — Chapter 15: Postmortem Culture: Learning from Failure** (sre.google/sre-book/postmortem-culture, free online) — the canonical treatment of blameless postmortems and why blame degrades the accuracy of incident learning.
- **"5 Whys" — Toyota Production System origins** (any solid overview of Taiichi Ohno's original technique) — useful for understanding 5 Whys' original manufacturing-process context, which clarifies both its strength (drilling past symptoms) and its limitation (assumes a single linear chain, less suited to complex, multi-causal software incidents without adaptation).
- **Etsy's "Blameless PostMortems and a Just Culture" (blog post by John Allspaw)** — one of the most-cited original arguments for blameless postmortems in the tech industry, and a good source of concrete language for framing a postmortem without assigning blame.

## Reference

- **Atlassian Incident Management Handbook** (atlassian.com/incident-management) — a free, practical reference covering severity levels, on-call rotation design, and runbook structure with real templates.
- **Incident.io blog / guides** — modern, concrete write-ups on escalation policy design and communication cadence during incidents, reflecting current industry practice at fast-moving tech companies.

## Practice

- **Today's lab** (write a real runbook + run a mock incident + conduct a mock postmortem) — the most direct way to internalize this day's material; treat the artifacts you produce as genuine interview prep and portfolio evidence.
- **PagerDuty's "Incident Response" training materials / on-call simulations** (response.pagerduty.com) — scenario-based exercises for practicing severity classification and escalation decisions outside of a real incident.
