# Day 112 — Resources: Platform Engineering Concepts

## Primary (assigned)

- **Backstage documentation** (backstage.io/docs) plus a **Team Topologies book summary** — the assigned starting point. Backstage's own docs directly cover the Catalog/TechDocs/Scaffolder mechanics; a summary of *Team Topologies* (Skelton & Pais) gives the organizational framing for where a platform team fits.

## Deepen your understanding

- **platformengineering.org** — the community reference site for platform engineering as a discipline, including the widely-cited definition of an Internal Developer Platform used throughout today's notes.
- **"Team Topologies" (Skelton & Pais, IT Revolution Press)** — the full book, if the summary leaves questions; especially useful for the platform-team-vs-enabling-team distinction and the "team API" concept.
- **Spotify Engineering's "Golden Paths" blog posts** — the original source of the "paved road" framing used in file 2; worth reading directly for the nuance around why Spotify deliberately avoided mandating adoption.
- **CNCF Platforms White Paper** (CNCF TAG App Delivery) — a vendor-neutral, community-authored definition of what an internal platform is and isn't, good for calibrating your own definition against an industry consensus.

## Reference / lookup

- **Backstage plugin directory** (backstage.io/plugins) — browse before building any custom internal UI; a large share of "we need custom platform tooling" needs already have a community plugin.
- **Backstage Software Templates reference** (backstage.io/docs/features/software-templates) — the authoritative schema for every Scaffolder action and template field used in today's lab.

## Practice

- **Backstage's own "Getting Started" tutorial** (backstage.io/docs/getting-started) — the fastest path from zero to a running local instance, directly feeding into today's lab.
- **roadmap.sh's Platform Engineer roadmap** — a free, community-maintained checklist of platform-engineering skills to benchmark your own progress against as you continue through this phase.
